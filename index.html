清浄な空間を保つ日本の作法

A Japanese Custom to Keep Spaces Clean

1. 空間の区別

1. Distinction of Space

日本には、建物の「外」と「内」を明確に区別し、屋内を特別な空間として扱う文化があります。

Japanese culture clearly distinguishes between the "outside" and "inside" of a building, treating the indoors as a special space.

2. 清潔さの維持

2. Maintaining Cleanliness

靴を脱ぐことは、外の汚れを持ち込まず、屋内の床や畳を清潔に保つための習慣です。

Taking off shoes is a custom to prevent bringing in outside dirt and to maintain the cleanliness of the floors and tatami mats.

3. 空間への敬意

3. Respect for the Space

靴を脱ぐという行為を通じて、その場所の歴史や空間に対する敬意を示しています。

The act of removing shoes demonstrates respect for the history and the space itself.

Hold Your Bowl, Honor the Meal 🍚

In Japan, holding your rice or soup bowl is a beautiful and natural dining etiquette. Here is why:

✨

Graceful Dining

Unlike using spoons, eating with chopsticks across a distance can lead to spills. Lifting the bowl closer to your mouth keeps your posture elegant and your table clean.

🙏

Respect the Craft

Holding the dishware is a gesture of appreciation. It shows your gratitude to the chef who prepared the meal and the artisan who crafted the bowl.
